Morse function


Let M be a smooth manifold. A critical point of a map u:M→ℝ at x∈M is called if the Hessian matrix Hu (in any local coordinate system) at x is non-degenerate.

A smooth function u:M→ℝ is called Morse if all its critical points are non-degenerate. Morse functions exist on any smooth manifold, and in fact form an open dense (http://planetmath.org/Dense) subset of smooth functions on M (this fact is often phrased “a generic smooth function is Morse”).
