centralizer


Let G a group acting on itself by conjugation. Let X be a subset of G. The stabilizer of X is called the centralizer of X and it’s the set

CG⁢(X)={g∈G:g⁢x⁢g-1=x for all ⁢x∈X}

For any group G, CG⁢(G)=Z⁢(G), the center of G. Thus, any subgroup of CG⁢(G) is an abelian subgroup of G. However, the converse is generally not true. For example, take any non-abelian group and pick any element not in the center. Then the subgroup generated by it is obviously abelian, clearly non-trivial and not contained in the center.
