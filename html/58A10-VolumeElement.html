volume element


If M is an n dimensional manifold, then a differential n form (http://planetmath.org/DifferentialForms) that is never zero is called a volume element or a volume form. Usually one volume form is associated with the manifold. The volume element is sometimes denoted by d⁢V, ω or voln. If the manifold is a Riemannian manifold with g, then the natural volume form is defined in local coordinates x1⁢…⁢xn by

d⁢V:=|g|⁢d⁢x1∧…∧d⁢xn.

It is not hard to show that a manifold has a volume form if and only if it is orientable.

If the manifold is ℝn, then the usual volume element d⁢V=d⁢x1∧d⁢x2∧…∧d⁢xn is called the Euclidean volume element or Euclidean volume form. In this context, ℂn is usually treated as ℝ2⁢n unless stated otherwise.

When n=2, then the form is frequently called the area element or area form and frequently denoted by d⁢A. Furthermore, when the manifold is a submanifold of ℝ3, then many authors will refer to a surface area element or surface area form.

When the context is measure theoretic, this form is sometimes called a volume measure, area measure, etc…
